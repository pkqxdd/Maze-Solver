Tuesday, October 17¶

I calculated the torque needed for each motor.

Mass of the robot: less than 100 grams, say 200 for safety measures. Powered by 2 motors. The wheels have radius of 3 cm (that’s just an arbitrary number) The friction coeffient of rubber on wood is 0.95 http://www2.mae.ufl.edu/designlab/Class%20Projects/Background%20Information/Friction%20coefficients.htm

Minimum torque needed:

\[\tau = F\cdot r = f \cdot r = c \cdot G \cdot r = cmgr\]

Where

  • \(F\) is the friction of the wheels
  • \(c\) is the friction coeffient, 0.95
  • \(G\) is the gravitational force on the robot
  • \(m\) is the mass of robot
  • \(g\) is the gravity constant
  • \(r\) is the radius of the wheel, 3

Therefore,

\[\begin{split}\tau &= 0.95 \cdot 0.2kg \cdot 9.8 N/kg \cdot 0.03 m\\ &=0.05586 N\cdot m = 7.9104425370524325 oz\cdot in\end{split}\]

Each motor should have a minium torque of 4 oz-in.
